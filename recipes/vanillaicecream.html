Homemade Vanilla Icecream

Rich and Creamy Homemade Vanilla Icecream

Indulge in the simple pleasure of crafting your own homemade vanilla ice cream with this delightful recipe. Made with love and a handful of wholesome ingredients, this creamy treat promises to satisfy your sweet cravings and captivate your taste buds with its rich, velvety texture and exquisite vanilla flavor.

Serve up a scoop or two in a crisp waffle cone or atop a warm slice of pie, letting the creamy goodness melt on your tongue and transport you to a place of pure bliss. With its timeless appeal and irresistible flavor, homemade vanilla ice cream is sure to become a cherished favorite for years to come.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 2/3 cup cane sugar
  • 2 tsps vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp sea salt

Directions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cream, milk, sugar, vanilla, and salt. Warm over medium-low heat, whisking often, for 5 minutes, or until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is warmed through.
  2. Remove the chilled ice cream base from the fridge and whisk well. Churn in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturerâ€™s instructions, typically 20 to 30 minutes. The ice cream will be soft after churning. For firmer ice cream, transfer to an airtight container and freeze for 2 to 4 hours before serving.
  3. Store in an airtight container in the freezer for up to a month. If the ice cream is too firm to scoop straight from the freezer, let sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften.
  4. Serve with desired toppings
